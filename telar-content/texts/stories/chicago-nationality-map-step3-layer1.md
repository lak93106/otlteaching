**Situated at the intersection of many railroads, Chicago served as a junction point for newly arrived migrants from the southern United States on the one hand, and newly arrived migrants on the other. Progressives like Jane Addams and pragmatic thinkers like sociologist Robert Park believed that creating maps that showed where people of different ethnicities and nationalities lived lwas a first step in understanding the cultural and ethnic background of residents in cities.**
